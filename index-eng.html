    Frequently Asked Questions

    Here are some frequently asked questions by our clients, and our extensive experience allows us to provide concise answers.

    • How to prepare for a meeting with a lawyer?

      Schedule a meeting with a lawyer in advance to reserve an appropriate amount of time. Gather and organize all documents related to your case, such as contracts, correspondence, evidence, bills, etc. Make a note of the most important dates, facts, and questions you want to ask the lawyer.

    • What are the costs of legal services?

      The costs of legal services depend on the type and complexity of the case, as well as the time and effort required to handle it. The amount of compensation is negotiated individually with each client, taking into account their financial situation and expectations. Various forms of billing are possible, such as flat fee, hourly rate, percentage of the dispute's value, or success bonus.

    • Can the law firm handle a case remotely?

      Yes, the law firm can handle cases remotely, without the need for a personal meeting with the client. In such cases, contact with the client is conducted via email, phone, or video conference. The client sends the necessary documents to the law firm electronically or by mail, and the law firm prepares and sends a power of attorney for the client to sign and return. Then the law firm represents the client in court or another authority and keeps the client informed about the progress of the case.

    • What are the specializations of the law firm?

      The law firm specializes in civil law, particularly inheritance law, law of obligations, family law, and labor law. The law firm also deals with criminal, administrative, and economic cases. The law firm provides services for both individuals and businesses. The law firm offers comprehensive legal assistance, including legal advice, drafting procedural documents, contracts, and other documents, representation in courts and administrative bodies, mediation, and negotiations.

    • What is a legal advisor, and how does it differ from a lawyer?

      The difference between a legal advisor and a lawyer mainly lies in the form of employment and the scope of representation in criminal and tax cases. Both professions, however, require high qualifications and experience in the field of law. The choice between them depends on the specific needs of the client.
